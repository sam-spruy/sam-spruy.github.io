Smart Home Energy Management Systems (EMS)

Smart homes are the future of technology. They use technological devices to ensure your home is up to your standards and how you want it to be.

In the UK, smart homes are experiencing unpredecented growth. Around 7.2 million households (24%) are already smart homes and projections indicate around 50% of UK households will use smart devices in homes by 2027.

The most popular devices in the home are voice-activated assistants (Amazon Alexa) found in 54% of homes. Another popular one are security cameras. Security systems have risen 20% just in 2023, as more people protect their property with surveillance and alarm systems.

The benefits of smart homes:

  • Make your life easier and convenient as you don't have to do manual chores.
  • Smart homes can help your house become energy efficient as it can analyse and adjust overconsumption.
  • You can manage your house remotely through smartphone or tablet. If you are at work or in the car, you can set the technology to check on pets or adjust the temperature.

Electric Vehicle (EV) Charging Stations

Electric vehicle charging stations are places where electric vehicles go and recharge their batteries. They supply electricity to the car through chargers.

There are different types of chargers: Level 1, Level 2 and Level 3.

  • Level 1 - These are standard chargers which take the most to charge up, more than 24 hours for a full battery.
  • Level 2 - Uses 240V and found in most public EV stations, taking around a few hours to charge.
  • Level 3 - Supplies car with a full battery charging under an hour.
